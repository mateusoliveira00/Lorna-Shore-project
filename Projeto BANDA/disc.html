Pain Remains I

Dancing Like Flames:

is about the longing for someone you’ve lost. The lyrics describe the feelings of being captivated by and drawn to someone, only for them to disappear in the blink of an eye. The chorus talks about dancing like flames flickering in the night, suggesting the idea of being in love with someone and burning brightly before fading away and melting away. The bridge speaks of a world without the person they love, while they recognize that they were meant to be together. The outro reflects on the state of the lover, questioning whether they are just a ghost and caught between two intertwining melodies. Overall, the song speaks of longing for someone who has gone away and hoping for them to return so the relationship can be recovered.

Pain Remains III

In a Sea of Fire

is a song about facing the consequences of one's actions. The song talks about dealing with the burden of guilt and how it can be a struggle to move on from that feeling. The song metaphorically speaks of throwing oneself into the "sea of fire", or burning away the past in order to emerge anew, unfettered by guilt and pain. The chorus serves as a conclusion, with the singer accepting that it is time to let go and say goodbye to the devil's choir in the form of a sea of fire. It speaks of a powerful moment of letting go, of leaving the stage and the burden of the past, and starting anew.
